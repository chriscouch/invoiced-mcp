Date Formats

Date format strings include alphabetic characters, which are translated according to the tables below. Non-alphabetic characters (i.e. dots, slashes) translate into themselves.

You can learn about more date characters here.


Example Format Strings

Month/Day/Year: 05/17/2014 
m/d/Y
Day/Month/Year: 17/05/2014 
d/m/Y
Month Day, Year: May 17, 2014 
F j, Y

Days

Character Description Example
j Day of the month without leading zeros 1 to 31
d Day of the month, 2 digits with leading zeros 01 to 31
l A full textual representation of the day of the week Sunday through Saturday
D A textual representation of a day, three letters Mon through Sun

Months

Character Description Example
n Numeric representation of a month, without leading zeros 1 through 12
m Numeric representation of a month, with leading zeros 01 through 12
F A full textual representation of a month, such as January or March January through December
M A short textual representation of a month, three letters Jan through Dec

Years

Character Description Example
Y A full numeric representation of a year, 4 digits 1999 or 2003
y A two digit representation of a year 99 or 03
